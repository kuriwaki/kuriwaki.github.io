My research focuses on democratic representation in American Politics. In an ongoing project, I am studying the structure of voter's choices across levels of government and the political economy of local elections, both using ballot image logs. Other projects involve public opinion measurement, survey methods, and applied statistics more generally. Prior to and during graduate school, I worked at the Analyst Institute in Washington D.C.

Ballot Image Log Project

The ballot image log project is collecting and organizing public ballot image log data (also known as Cast Voter Records) to advance the understanding of voting patterns in federal, state and local elections. One of the project's goals is to establish a relational database for such data. This project is one of the 2018 New Initiative Grants from the MIT Election Data and Science Lab and the Madison Initiative of the Hewlett Foundation. See the associated paper of this ongoing work here. More information about the queryable database, which is currently in development with help from collaborators, is at this page. Please contact me for any questions.
