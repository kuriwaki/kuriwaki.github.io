My research focuses on democratic representation in American Politics. In an ongoing project, I am studying the structure of voter's choices across levels of government and the political economy of local elections, both through the use of cast vote records. My other interests include public opinion, survey methods, and applied statistics more generally. Prior to and during graduate school, I worked at the Analyst Institute in Washington D.C.

Ballot Image Log Project

The ballot image log project is collecting and organizing public ballot image log data (also known as Cast Voter Records) to advance the understanding of voting patterns in federal, state and local elections. One of the project's goals is to establish a relational database for such data. This project is one of the 2018 New Initiative Grants from the MIT Election Data and Science Lab. See the associated paper of this ongoing work here. More information about the queryable database, which is currently in development with help from collaborators, is at this page. Please feel free to contact me for any questions.

Datasets

  • The Cumulative CCES Common Content (2006-2018) is a part of the Cooperative Congressional Election Survey Dataverse. It combines all common content respondents of the CCES and harmonizes key variables, so that researchers can analyze all years of the CCES or merge in standardized variables with their own CCES datasets.
