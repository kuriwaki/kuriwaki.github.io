Welcome.

Shiro Kuriwaki is a graduate student in Harvard's department of Government since 2015. His research uses quantitative, psychological, and empirical tools to answer how American democracy functions in practice. Prior to graduate school, he worked at the Analyst Institute in Washington D.C., where he took part in implementing field experiments during the 2014 U.S. election cycle. During the 2016 election cycle, he is working with the Analyst Institute in a part-time capacity, specializing in machine learning models of heterogenous treatment effects.
